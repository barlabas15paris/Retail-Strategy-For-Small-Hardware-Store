# Module 1 – Retail Management Summary

## Store Saloon Overview
1. Strategic plan is in place
2. Move to managerial aspects
3. Coordinate day-to-day tactical actions

### Key Aspects of Retail Decisions
- **Product assortment**: type of product lines, number of lines, brands to carry, type of customer service (high contact → self-service)
- **Merchandise breadth**: product line width, how products relate to each other in a retail environment, balance between breadth and depth
- **Customer support**: credit terms, delivery services, after-sales service
- **Presentation**: attractiveness quotient
- **Marketing**: relational marketing (developing strong customer relationships)

## Strategic Retail Management Insights
**3 factors that differentiate retail from other businesses:**
1. Average transaction amount is lower than for manufacturers  
2. Final consumers make many unplanned or impulse purchases  
3. >90% of transactions occur in stores and will continue to do so

**6 Steps to Develop a Retail Strategy:**
1. Define the type of business (goods/service category, full service or no-frills)  
2. Set long- and short-term objectives (sales, profit, market share, image)  
3. Determine the target consumer market:
   - Gender
   - Income level
   - Brand preferences  
4. Devise a long-term plan for business direction and employees  
5. Implement a strategy combining factors like store location, product assortment, pricing, advertising, and displays  
6. Evaluate performance and correct weaknesses promptly

---

## Retail Concept Principles
1. **Customer orientation** – satisfy customer needs to the fullest  
2. **Coordinated effort** – integrate all plans and activities efficiently  
3. **Value-driven** – good value to consumers (upscale or discount) with prices appropriate to product/service level  
4. **Goal orientation** – set goals and use strategy to achieve them  

> Essentially, communicate with shoppers, understand their desires, maintain a consistent strategy (designer brands, sufficient sales personnel, attractive displays, appropriate pricing).

---

## Value-Oriented Retail Strategy
- **Expected Value** – minimum value chain elements for a customer segment  
- **Augmented Value** – extra elements that differentiate one retailer from another  
- **Potential Value** – value elements not yet perfected by competitors  

### Common Pitfalls
1. Planning value only from a price perspective  
2. Providing services customers do not want or pay extra for  
3. Competing in the wrong value/price segment  
4. Believing augmented elements alone create value  
5. Paying lip service to customer service  

### Customer Relationships
- Main reason for building relationships: economic  
- Key attributes: **Trust** and **Commitment**
