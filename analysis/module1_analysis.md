# Module 1 – Retail Management Analysis

This file contains the analysis of Module 1 notes, applied to my shop.

---

## 1. Store Overview

**Original Notes:**
1. Strategic plan is in place  
2. Then turn to more managerial aspects  
3. Coordinate day-to-day tactical actions  

**Analysis for My Shop:**
- **Strategic plan:** Already identified main product categories, stock priorities, and high-demand items.  
- **Managerial aspects:** Need to organize order processes, stock replenishment, invoicing.  
- **Day-to-day actions:** Define staff responsibilities per section, plan daily restocking and promotion schedules, identify processes to automate.

---

## 2. Key Aspects

**Original Notes:**
- Product assortment: product lines, brands, type of customer service  
- Merchandise breadth/depth: balance between variety and depth  
- Customer support: credit terms, delivery, after-sales service  
- Presentation: attractiveness quotient  
- Marketing: relational marketing  

**Analysis for My Shop:**
- **Product assortment:** Core categories = tools, paints, DIY accessories. Decide number of brands per category (e.g., 3-5 main brands).  
- **Merchandise breadth/depth:** Focus on few categories but sufficient stock per category.  
- **Customer support:** Credit for professional clients, delivery for large orders, after-sales service for big projects.  
- **Presentation:** Store is clean, easy to navigate, visually attractive.  
- **Marketing / relational marketing:** Maintain relationships with WhatsApp updates, small loyalty program, project tips.

---

## 3. Value-Oriented Retail Strategy

**Original Notes:**
- Expected / Augmented / Potential value  
- Pitfalls: price-only focus, unwanted services, wrong segment, lip service, believing augmented value alone creates value  

**Analysis for My Shop:**
- **Expected Value:** Basic tools & paints always available, correct advice for customers.  
- **Augmented Value:** DIY project kits, quick color-mixing, personalized advice.  
- **Potential Value:** Online ordering & pick-up, QR guides for DIY projects, simple CRM for recommendations.  
- **Pitfalls:**  
  - Do not focus only on price—cannot compete with big discount stores.  
  - Avoid services customers don’t want.  
  - Avoid targeting professional + DIY segments without clear strategy.  
  - Augmented elements alone do not build value; must be meaningful.  

---

## 4. Customer Relationships

**Original Notes:**
- Main reason: economic  
- Key attributes: trust & commitment  

**Analysis for My Shop:**
- **Trust:** Customers must know essential products will always be in stock; honest advice on what they need.  
- **Commitment:** Consistent product quality, stable staff, follow-up on projects and orders.
